Day 1

Chengdu arrival, welcome briefing, insurance and gear check

Clients land in Chengdu and meet the coordinator. We confirm passport details, emergency contact, insurance coverage, fitness notes, clothing layers, boots, gloves, headlamp, and personal medication. This creates trust before the mountain begins.

Day 2

Transfer to Sichuan highlands and approach the mountain area

The drive becomes part of the experience: snow peaks, valleys, Tibetan villages, and photo stops. On arrival, guides brief the route, check weather, and prepare the team for an early alpine start.

Day 3

Base camp movement, snow skills, and summit preparation

Move closer to the peak and practice crampon movement, pacing, hydration, layering, and turnaround rules. The marketing message is simple: this is not a random hike, it is a guided snow mountain climb.

Day 4

Summit attempt, safe descent, celebration, and return plan

Start before sunrise for the best window. Guides manage safety, timing, summit photos, and descent discipline. After the climb, clients receive next-step support for Chengdu return, hotel extension, or onward flights.
